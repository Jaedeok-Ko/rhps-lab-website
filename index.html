About the Lab

Who We Are

The Refrigeration and Heat Pump Systems (RHPS) Laboratory at Korea Maritime & Ocean University is led by Assistant Professor Jaedeok Ko. Our research focuses on the fundamentals and applications of vapor compression refrigeration and heat pump systems, with particular emphasis on natural refrigerants, energy efficiency, and sustainable thermal engineering.

We investigate household refrigerator-freezers, electric vehicle air-conditioning, CO₂ transcritical systems, two-phase flow phenomena, and advanced heat exchanger design — combining experimental investigation with computational modeling to deliver practical, high-impact solutions.

We collaborate closely with industry partners such as LG Electronics and national research programs funded by the National Research Foundation of Korea, bridging fundamental science and real-world thermal engineering challenges.

Research & Projects

Areas of Investigation

❄️

Refrigeration Systems

Household refrigerator-freezer performance, capillary tube behavior, non-equilibrium two-phase flow, and fault diagnostics using natural refrigerants.

♨️

Heat Pump Systems

Environment-friendly air-to-water heat pump systems operating with natural refrigerant R-290 for domestic hot water and space heating applications.

🚗

EV Thermal Management

Vapor compression air conditioning and heat pump systems for electric vehicles, including CO₂ transcritical cycles and start-up transient behavior.

🔁

CO₂ Transcritical Cycles

Energy-saving strategies through heat rejection pressure management and cascaded cycle configurations using CO₂ as a natural refrigerant.

🔩

Heat Exchangers

Advanced heat exchangers including TPMS structures, brazed plate heat exchangers, and capillary tube–suction line heat exchangers.

💧

Two-Phase Flow & Heat Transfer

Refrigerant distribution, foam flow friction, and moisture evaporation models to improve thermal system performance and predictive accuracy.
